Introduction

It has long been thought that thermodynamics and evolution go against each other: the Second Law of thermodynamics holds that the universe, as a closed system, tends to be more disorder: perfume diffuses and the broken mirror will never be reunited. From this perspective, it is amazing how life and society emerge and sustain themselves. This article introduces a bold assumption that the Second law of thermodynamics is not in conflict with evolution, but rather the underlying principle of evolution. Living systems and societies that stand out from evolution are those that consume energy and produce disorder most efficiently.

Cheklist

Table 1. The Final Project Checklist for Students from INFO VIZ class.

1. Title
Short, powerful, and creative.

2. Figure
Eye-catching, both scientific and arty, all shapes and colors should serve for a purpose.

3. Legend
All functional shapes and colors need to be explained.

4. Significance
Why this visualization matters to our society?

5. Findings
What unknowns are turned into knowns?

6. Data
Large-volume (one million or more records), long-term (three or more years) data on human behaviors. Expensive actions (education, work, marriage, migration) are preferable to cheap actions (all kinds of social media engagements). Recommendation: Burning Glass (BG), Open Syllabus Project (OSP), Microsoft Academic Graph (MAG). The accessibility to BG and OSP will be arranged carefully to protect the benefits of the institution providers.

7. Method
Only non-trivial methods used need to be explained

8. Discussion
The big, inspiring questions remaining unsolved

*9. Story
Personal stories as the motivation to this research. Optional.
